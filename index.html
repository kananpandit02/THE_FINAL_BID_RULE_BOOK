Event Overview

THE FINAL BID is a flagship strategic cricket auction simulation event conducted as part of PERCEPTRON 2026. The event is designed to replicate the intensity, complexity, and decision-making challenges of a real-world professional cricket league auction.

Participants take on the role of franchise management teams and compete in a live, offline, open-bidding auction to build a complete cricket squad from a predefined player pool. Each decision made during the auction directly affects team balance, budget sustainability, and final scoring outcomes.

The primary objective of the event is to evaluate participants on strategic planning, cricketing knowledge, analytical ability, financial management, risk assessment, and real-time adaptability under strict constraints and competitive pressure.

Teams must carefully manage a fixed auction purse while ensuring compliance with mandatory squad composition rules, overseas player limits, and role-based requirements. Aggressive bidding without foresight can compromise squad balance, while overly conservative strategies may result in missed opportunities.

Unlike conventional quizzes or games, THE FINAL BID rewards long-term vision over individual picks. Teams must anticipate opponent behavior, assess player value beyond base price, and plan for both the final 15-player squad and the optimal Playing XI.

After the auction concludes, teams submit their Playing XI, captain, and wicketkeeper selections. Final scores are calculated using a points-based evaluation system that includes base player performance values and hidden strategic bonuses.

The team with the highest total points is declared the winner, followed by the runner-up and third-place teams. In the event of a tie, predefined tie-breaking criteria are applied to ensure fair and transparent results.

THE FINAL BID is not merely an auction‚Äîit is a test of leadership, strategy, and decision-making under pressure, offering participants a realistic experience of franchise-level cricket management.

Squad Composition Rules

  • Each team must complete the auction with a FINAL SQUAD of EXACTLY 15 players.
  • The 15-player squad must mandatorily include the following minimum role-based composition:

    • At least 4 Bowlers

    • At least 2 Wicketkeepers
  • A maximum of 7 overseas (non-Indian) players is allowed in the 15-player squad.
  • Each player shall be counted under only one role, as defined in the official player pool. Dual-role interpretation is strictly prohibited.
  • The total spending of a team must remain within the ‚Çπ90 Crore budget at all times.
  • Teams are fully responsible for managing their budget and bids in such a manner that completion of the 15-player squad remains possible.
  • Failure to satisfy ANY squad composition rule ‚Äî including player count, role requirements, overseas limit, or budget constraints ‚Äî shall result in IMMEDIATE DISQUALIFICATION.

Playing XI Rules

  • Each team must submit a Playing XI of EXACTLY 11 players selected from their finalized 15-player squad.
  • The Playing XI must mandatorily satisfy the following composition rules:
    • At least 3 Batsmen
    • At least 3 Bowlers
    • At least 2 All-rounders
    • At least 1 Wicketkeeper
  • A maximum of 4 overseas (non-Indian) players is permitted in the Playing XI.
  • Each selected player shall be counted under only one role, as defined in the official player pool.
  • The Playing XI shall be evaluated for validity before points calculation.
  • Any Playing XI that violates ANY of the above conditions shall be declared INVALID.
  • An INVALID Playing XI shall result in:
    • No points being calculated
    • DISQUALIFICATION from scoring

Scoring & Tie-Breakers

  • Team scores shall be calculated ONLY after submission of a VALID Playing XI.
  • If a Playing XI is declared INVALID, the team shall receive NO SCORE and shall be disqualified from ranking.
  • The total team score shall be calculated in the following strict order:
    1. Sum of Base Points of all 11 players in the Playing XI
    2. Addition of Captain Bonus Points
    3. Addition of Wicketkeeper Bonus Points
    4. Addition of Star Player Bonus Points
  • Final rankings shall be determined based on total points obtained.
  • The team with the highest total score shall be declared the WINNER, followed by RUNNER-UP and THIRD PLACE.
  • In the event of a tie in total points, the following tie-breaker rules shall be applied sequentially:
    1. Total points of reserve (non-playing) players
    2. Remaining purse balance after completion of the auction
  • If a tie still persists after applying all tie-breakers, the final decision shall rest with the Organizing Committee.
